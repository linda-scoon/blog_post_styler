Mbayani market is located in Blantyre, not far from Chirimba Market. It serves the surrounding communities of Nyambadwe and Chemusa and is well known for its cheap products, ranging from fresh fruits and vegetables to secondhand clothes. It is one of the most popular markets in Blantyre and can get very congested, especially in the evening.

How to get to Mbayani Market

You can travel to Mbayani market by minibus, taxi, or your own car.

To get there by minibus from Blantyre CBD, catch a bus from Kandodo bus stop heading to Chileka via the M1 road and get off at the Mbayani market bus stop, which is very close to the market. It takes about 5 minutes, then a minute’s walk down to the market. Expect to pay K400 for a return minibus fare.

By taxi it takes about 2 minutes and costs around K2,000 return. There is no parking area, so if you use your own car you park anywhere at the market at your own risk.

Things you can buy at Mbayani Market

Tomatoes & OnionsCheap, good-quality — what Mbayani is best known for.
VegetablesA wide variety of fresh fruits and vegetables at very cheap prices.
ClothesSecondhand traders with the cheapest prices of any Blantyre market.
FishBoth fresh and dried fish are sold here.
