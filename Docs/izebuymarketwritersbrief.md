# izebuy Markets — Writer's Brief

**What we're doing:** We are mapping the markets of Malawi. A market isn't a tidy
place a map app can capture — but it has its own order. The fish stalls are always
on one side; the people change daily, the layout doesn't. Your job is to go to a
market and explain *what is where*, so that a complete stranger could read your
article and walk in knowing exactly where to go. We publish these on izebuy because
no one else bothers to record them — and we do.

**Target length: about 2,000 words.** Not padding — real, observed detail. If you
follow every prompt below, you'll reach it naturally.

---

## How to write it (read this once, it matters)

- Write in **Microsoft Word**, plain document. **Do not** paste from websites or add
  any formatting tricks — just type.
- Use Word's **Heading styles** for the section titles below: **Heading 1** for the
  title, **Heading 2** for each main section, **Heading 3** for sub-sections inside
  the walk-through. (Home tab → the "Styles" gallery → "Heading 2", etc.) This is
  how our system lays the page out — if you just make text big and bold instead of
  using the Heading style, it won't work.
- **Use the exact section headings listed below, in this order.** You may add
  Heading 3 sub-sections inside the walk-through, but keep the main Heading 2
  sections as they are.
- Write in your own voice, as if telling a friend who's never been. First person is
  welcome ("when I went…").
- **Don't invent facts.** If you're not sure something is true, either leave it out
  or say who told you ("traders say…", "locals call it…").

---

## Photos (the photos ARE the article — take them seriously)

- **Landscape orientation** (phone held sideways), good light, in focus.
- **One clear subject per photo** — the thing you're describing.
- **Name each photo file after what it shows**, in lower case with hyphens:
  - The shop or stall name → `lsm-general-dealers.jpg`, `faith-salon.jpg`
  - The section it belongs to → `getting-to-mbayani.jpg`, `the-fish-side.jpg`
  - The main cover photo → `mbayani-market.jpg`
  - Your own photo → `about-the-author.jpg`
  - The hand-drawn map → `mbayani-map.jpg`
- **Pick one best photo as the cover.** Name it after the market itself.
- **Draw a simple map** of the market by hand (paper and pen is fine), photograph it
  clearly, and include it. Show the main paths, which side has what, and the
  entrance. It doesn't need to be neat — it needs to be useful.
- **People:** get a nod of consent before photographing someone's face or stall up
  close. If they'd rather not, photograph the goods, not the person.
- Aim for **8–15 usable photos** across the article.

## The sections to write

### Heading 1 — The market's name
Just the name, e.g. **Mbayani Market**. Nothing else.

---

### (Opening — no heading) — 2 to 3 lines
Straight in: what this market is and where it is. One or two sentences a stranger
would need. *Example: "Mbayani is a busy second-hand-clothes market in Blantyre,
serving Nyambadwe and Chemusa. It's cheap, crowded, and best known for bales."*
**~120–180 words.**

---

### Heading 2 — Getting to [Market] from [nearest town]
Assume the reader knows the **nearest big town** (e.g. Blantyre) but not the market.
Cover, in bullet-style prose:
- Which bus/minibus stop to start from, which route, where to get off.
- How long it takes and the **fare** (return).
- The same for **taxi** (time + fare).
- **By car:** is there parking? Is it safe? Where do people actually park?
- Any landmark to look for so they know they've arrived.
**~180–250 words.**

---

### Heading 2 — The story of [place]  *(optional, but try)*
**Local** history — not Wikipedia. The kind only someone from there would know:
- How long the market has been there; who remembers it small.
- A local landmark and its story ("the road by the school", "the old tree", "the
  shortcut everyone uses").
- A warning or habit locals have ("don't use that alley after dark", "the main road
  is long, locals cut through the side street").

If you genuinely don't know any local history, **skip this section** — don't make it
up. If you heard it from someone, say so ("traders say…"). **~150–300 words.**

---

### Heading 2 — The feel of the place
Make the reader feel they've been there:
- The **weather / climate** — hot and dusty? cool? rainy season mud?
- The **atmosphere** — loud, calm, packed in the evening, sleepy in the morning?
- A **fun fact** about the town or market, *if it's true* — anything local, even if
  it's small ("the same river that runs past here reaches all the way to the lake").
  Honest and small beats impressive and invented.
**~200–300 words.**

---

### Heading 2 — What [Market] is famous for
Every market has a character. Some are *the* clothes market, some are *the* fish
market, some are just good general markets. Say which, clearly:
- **What it's known for** — the one thing people go there specifically to buy.
- Why you'd choose **this** market over another for that thing (price? choice?
  freshness?).
- What else you can get there, even if it's not famous for it.
**~200–300 words.**

---

### Heading 2 — A walk through the market  *(the heart of the article)*
This is the mapping. Walk the reader through it as if leading them by the hand. Use
**Heading 3** sub-sections for each area/zone, e.g.:

> #### The clothes side
> #### The fresh-produce side
> #### The fish section
> #### The food stalls

For each zone:
- Where it is relative to the entrance ("turn left at the gate, it's the second
  row").
- What's sold there, roughly how many stalls, and what prices to expect.
- A photo of that zone (named after it).
- Any tip ("this row is cheapest", "go early before the good bales are gone").

Reference your hand-drawn **map** here. This section should be your **longest**.
**~400–600 words.**

---

### Heading 2 — Shops, services & opening hours
The fixed shops and services (not the daily stalls). For **each one**, give:
- Its **name**.
- What it sells / does, in a line.
- **Opening days and hours.**
- A **photo named after the shop** (e.g. `first-quality-designs.jpg`).

Include services too — mills, barbershops, salons, phone/airtime, etc.
**~150–300 words.**

---

### Heading 2 — Practical tips
Quick, useful, scannable:
- **Best time to go** overall, and specifically for **fresh food**.
- When it's **too crowded** to bother.
- Your **favourite stall or shop** and why.
- Anything you wish you'd known before your first visit.
**~150–250 words.**

---

### Heading 2 — Our honest take
**No star ratings.** Just tell the reader, honestly, what it's like — describe it so
they can judge for themselves:
- **Value** — is it actually cheap, and is the quality decent for the price?
- **Cleanliness** — describe what you saw (litter, drainage, smell) plainly.
- **Safety** — describe your *own* experience ("I kept my bag in front in the busy
  aisles", "I parked outside and the car was fine"). General crowd advice is good.
  **Never** accuse a named shop or person of theft or wrongdoing.
- Would you go back? Who is this market right for?
**~200–300 words.**

---

### Heading 2 — About the Author  *(always the last section)*
A short bio, written by you, in third person:
- Your name, where you're based, what you do.
- A line of personality (what you're into).
- How to reach you (email).
- Include **your photo**, named `about-the-author.jpg`.
**~60–100 words.**

---

## Before you submit — the checklist
- [ ] Title is in **Heading 1**; every section uses the **Heading 2** style (and
      Heading 3 inside the walk-through).
- [ ] Sections are in the **order above**, with **About the Author last**.
- [ ] Around **2,000 words**, and the walk-through is the longest part.
- [ ] **8–15 landscape photos**, each **named after what it shows**.
- [ ] One cover photo named after the market; one hand-drawn map; one author photo.
- [ ] No invented facts; anything uncertain is attributed ("locals say…").
- [ ] No named shop or person accused of anything in the safety section.
- [ ] Written in plain Word — no pasted web content, no manual formatting tricks.

## What NOT to do
- Don't write vague filler to reach the word count — write more *observed detail*.
- Don't copy history or descriptions from other websites.
- Don't position or resize photos yourself — just drop them in the right section;
  the system arranges them.
- Don't add tables, text boxes, coloured text or fancy fonts — they're stripped out.
