Mbayani market is located in Blantyre not far from Chirimba Market. It serves the surrounding communities of Nyambadwe and Chemusa and is well known for its offering of cheap products ranging from fresh fruits and vegetables to secondhand clothes.

Mbayani market is one of the popular markets in Blantyre and can get very congested, especially in the evening.

How to get to Mbayani Market

You can travel to Mbayani market by minibus, taxi, or your own car.

To get to the market by minibus from Blantyre CBD, you need to catch a bus from Kandodo bus stop heading to Chileka via the M1 road and get off at Mbayani market bus stop, which is very close to the market.

It takes approximately 5 minutes to get there and then it’s only a minute’s walk down to the market. You should expect to pay K400 for a return minibus fare.

If you decide to use a taxi, it should take you about 2 minutes and you should expect to pay K2,000 for a return taxi fare.

Unfortunately, there is no parking area, so if you decide to use your own car you can park anywhere at the market at your own risk.

Things you can buy at Mbayani Market

Tomatoes and Onions: Mbayani market is where you can find cheap and good quality tomatoes and onions.

Vegetables: The market has a variety of fresh fruits and vegetables, sold at exceptionally cheap prices.

Clothes: Mbayani has numerous secondhand clothes traders who have the cheapest prices of all the markets in Blantyre.

Fish: You can also buy fresh and dried fish.

Shops and services you can find at Mbayani Market

Services

Mill: There is one unnamed maize mill which serves people around the market.

Barbershops: There are lots of barbershops — Kelvins Barbershop, Angoni Barbershop and Pachil Barbershop.

Hair Dressing Salons: There are also many salons. One of them is Faith Salon and Cosmetics, which offers braiding and hair relaxing.

Shops

LSM General Dealers: Plastic kitchenware. Open Mon–Sat, 7am to 6pm.

Mutheya Devoleyaka Shopping Centre: Grocery store. Open weekdays, 5am to 8pm.

First Quality Designs: They sew and sell clothes. Open Mon–Sat, 8am to 6pm.

Final review of the Mbayani Market

Price ●●●●● 5/5 — everything is very cheap but still good quality compared to the Blantyre CBD flea market.
Cleanliness ●●●●● 0/5 — the market is not kept clean; litter could be a health hazard.
